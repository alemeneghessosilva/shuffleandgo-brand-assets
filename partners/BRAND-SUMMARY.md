# Shuffle and Go — Brand Summary (1-pager)

## Identity

**Shuffle and Go** is a competitive trading-card-game channel (Star Wars Unlimited, Riftbound) that frames the tournament circuit as a travel show.

- **Founder/host:** Alexandre ("Alê") — São Paulo, Brazil.
- **Premise:** *"I travel because I compete."*
- **Tagline:** *"Cards in Hand, World Ahead"* (never used alone as a logo — always paired with the wordmark).
- **Handle:** `@shuffleandgo` (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok).
- **Category position:** competitive TCG **+** travel — the unclaimed intersection.

The brand sits closer to an old library or a leather passport than a neon gaming brand. Adult, considered, story-first.

## Audience rings

- **Core:** Competitive TCG players (SWU, Riftbound, adjacent).
- **Adjacent:** Casual / TCG-curious / lapsed players.
- **Crossover:** Travel + lifestyle viewers, "follow a person" fans.
- **Golden rule:** *stakes over mechanics* — never let game jargon become the gatekeeper.

## The three content pillars

Every piece of content serves at least one, ideally two:

1. **The Competition (40%)** — tournaments, decks, key decisions, the meta
2. **The Journey (40%)** — airports, cities, food, venues, people met along the way
3. **The Person (20%)** — Alê's honest reactions, prep, doubt, joy

## Voice

Competitive-traveler. Chill but thorough. Honest > polished. First-person, present-tense narration in Alê's real voice (Brazilian-accented English by default; Portuguese flips in for Brazil-facing moments).

**Lead with stakes, not mechanics:**
- ✅ *"I needed my opponent to not have the one card that could stop me."*
- ❌ *"I played a resource into reserve."*

**Specificity wins:**
- ✅ *"Suzhou Regional Open"*
- ❌ *"a tournament in China"*

## Hard nos (do not improvise around these)

1. Pure black `#000000` as a default background — Forest Green is the brand's "black."
2. Red, blue, purple, neon, saturated gaming colors as primary.
3. More than 4 words on a thumbnail.
4. More than 6 short lines in a caption.
5. Creator-default openers ("Hey guys, welcome back...").
6. AI voiceover or fake-pro narrator voice — Alê's real voice (with accent) is non-negotiable.
7. Gamer fonts, slab serifs, novelty display fonts, Star Wars title fonts, hand-lettering.
8. Game-mechanics jargon where stakes language would land harder.
9. Logo manipulation — see `LOGO-USAGE.md`.
10. Cabinet Grotesk under 14pt.
11. Tagline used in isolation as a logo.
12. Unearned hype claims — "champion," "World," "Galactic" are reserved for actual results.
